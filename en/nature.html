🌿 Nature and local area

Gravassryggen is surrounded by beautiful mountain nature, lakes, hiking terrain and rich wildlife.

⛰ The mountain landscape

The cabin is located in scenic surroundings with views towards mountains and water.

The area is well suited for quiet walks, longer mountain hikes and nature experiences all year round.

🌸 The seasons in Sirdal

Each season has its own charm.

  • Spring: Nature comes back to life and the mountains turn green.
  • Summer: Long bright days, great hiking, swimming and fishing.
  • Autumn: Clear days, beautiful autumn colours, fresh mountain air and berries in the terrain.
  • Winter: Snow-covered mountains, skiing, sledging and atmospheric winter evenings.

🌦 Mountain safety and weather

The weather in the mountains can change quickly, even in summer.

We recommend bringing windproof clothing, an extra sweater or jacket, and good shoes if you are going hiking.

Check the weather forecast before longer trips, and adapt your route to the weather, conditions and your experience.

🫐 Berry picking

Late summer and autumn offer good opportunities for picking berries in the area around the cabin.

  • Blueberries – usually from late July through August.
  • Cloudberries – usually from late July to mid-August, depending on weather and altitude.
  • Lingonberries – usually from late August and into autumn.

The berry season varies from year to year, but when conditions are good, you can find berries in many places around Gravassryggen.

Feel free to bring a small bucket or bag on your walk.

🍄 Mushrooms

In late summer and autumn, you may find several edible mushrooms in the area around Gravassryggen.

Chanterelles generally prefer terrain slightly lower than the cabin, but they can often be found in the forested areas further down the valley.

⚠️ If you do not have experience with mushrooms, we recommend leaving them. Norway has several poisonous mushrooms, and some species are deadly. Only pick and eat mushrooms you are completely certain are edible.

🦌 Wildlife

The area has rich wildlife. If you are lucky, you may see moose, red fox, hare, badger, stoat, black grouse or golden eagle.

Please respect the animals and enjoy these experiences from a distance.

🌿 The right to roam

In Norway, everyone may walk freely in uncultivated land and pick wild berries, mushrooms and flowers for personal use.

Please respect nature, close gates behind you and take all waste home.

🌼 Take care of nature

We want the area to remain just as beautiful for future visitors.

Bring all rubbish with you, respect plants and wildlife, and follow marked paths where appropriate.

📵 Peace and quiet

One of the greatest qualities of Gravassryggen is the silence.

Enjoy the view from the terrace, the sounds of nature and the clear starry sky in the evening.

📷 Capture the moments

Sirdal offers many wonderful photo opportunities – from mountain lakes and hiking trails to sunsets and winter landscapes.

We hope you take many good memories home with you.
