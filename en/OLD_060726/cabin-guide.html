💧 Drinking water

The cabin has fresh well water from the mountains.

The water is of very good quality and is safe to drink straight from the tap.

🚿 Water use

The cabin area uses a shared mountain well with clean, good drinking water.

Please use water thoughtfully. Avoid unnecessary water use and keep showers to a reasonable length so there is plenty of water available throughout your stay.

Thank you for helping us take care of this important natural resource.

⚠️ 🚽 Toilet

This is very important.

To avoid blocked pipes and costly damage, only toilet paper must be flushed down the toilet.

Wet wipes, paper towels, cotton buds, sanitary products, tampons, pads, food waste and all other waste must be put in the bin.

If the pipes become blocked due to incorrect use, this may lead to significant costs and affect both your stay and future guests.

🔥 Heating

The cabin is heated with electric panel heaters and underfloor heating.

In spring, summer and autumn, this is normally sufficient to keep a comfortable temperature, and the fireplace is therefore not available for use.

If you visit the cabin in winter, you will find information about using the fireplace and lighting instructions through the link below.

🗑 Waste disposal

There is no ordinary waste collection for cabins in Sirdal.

All waste must therefore be taken with you and delivered to a local waste station during your stay or before departure.

The nearest waste station is by the entrance to Sirdal Skisenter, and most guests pass it when shopping or going out for activities. It is therefore practical to bring the waste at the same time.

See all waste stations in the interactive map.

📷 Security cameras

There are three outdoor security cameras on the property.

All cameras are switched off during guests’ stays.

🦊 Wildlife

The area around the cabin has rich wildlife.

If you are lucky, you may see moose, red fox, hare, black grouse, badger, stoat or golden eagle.

Enjoy nature, respect wildlife and keep a good distance if you encounter wild animals.
